Why is it important to learn about the universe?

Curiosity fuels discovery, which, in turn, fuels knowledge. By asking questions about our universe, we are allowing ourselves to discover new information that could give us a deeper understanding of our origins. So, without further ado, here are one hundred mind-blowing space facts that will leave you in wonder!

Facts About Astronomical Terminology

1.One “light minute” is the distance light travels in 60 seconds. This is equivalent to 11,160,000 miles (17,960,279 kilometers).
2.One “astronomical unit” (AU) is the average distance from the center of the Earth to the center of the Sun. This is equivalent to 93 million miles (149.6 million kilometers).
3.The speed of light is 299,792,458 meters per second, and it is the fastest known thing in the universe.
4.“Universal Time,” also known as “Greenwich Mean Time,” is measured by the stars position instead of clocks.
5.An “exoplanet” is a planet discovered outside of our solar system.

Facts About Space-Based Professions

1.“Astronomy” is the study of stars, galaxies, and other elements in the universe.
2.“Astrophysics” is the study of the math and theories behind the things that exist in the universe.
3.“Cosmology” is the study of the origins of the universe– how it began, where it came from, etc.
4.“Astrology” is a pseudoscience that focuses on the effect celestial bodies have on humans and their lives.
5.The average American astronaut makes about $140,000 USD a year.
6.Astronauts must prepare for a decade before going into space. This decade includes basic training, education, and practice runs.


General Facts About the Universe

2.Every galaxy has a supermassive black hole at its center.
3.The universe is thought to be around 13.7 billion years old.
4.The universe is expanding–it increases by 0.007% in one million years.
